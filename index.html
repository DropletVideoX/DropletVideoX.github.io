Abstract


Spatio-temporal consistency is a critical research topic in video generation. A qualified generated video segment must ensure plot plausibility and coherence while maintaining visual consistency of objects and scenes across varying viewpoints.Prior research, especially open-source projects, primarily focuses on either temporal or spatial consistency, or their basic combination, such as concurrent plot generation and camera control. In this paper, we explore integral spatio-temporal consistency, a more intricate challenge. The model must account for inter-frame influence, the synergy between plot progression and camera techniques, and the long-term impact of prior content on subsequent generation. Our work spans from dataset development to the application of advanced methodologies. Initially, we compiled DropletVideo-10M, a comprehensive dataset for spatio-temporal consistency in video generation.This dataset contains 10 million videos with dynamic camera movements and object actions. Each video is paired with an average caption of 206 words.Subsequently, we developed and trained DropletVideo, a video generation model adept at maintaining spatio-temporal consistency.




DropletVideo-10M Dataset

DropletVideo-10M dataset presents a novel avenue for the investigation of comprehensive spatio-temporal consistency in video generation. It comprises 10 million spatio-temporal videos, each accompanied by an average caption length of 206 words. When contrasted with the Panda70M dataset, the captions in our videos demonstrate a significantly higher degree of spatio-temporal information, as indicated by the highlighted text in red.

The overall framework of DropletVideo . The top left section includes the differences between the conventional sampling strategy (a) and our motion controllable strategy (b). Integral Spatio-temporal Consistency

DropletVideo focuses on integral spatio-temporal consistency during video generation. It addresses the spatial distortion issues caused by camera movement, ensuring smooth plot progression during camera movement and the spatio-temporal consistency of objects within the scene. More importantly, in the development of a video scenario, the emerging scenes do not affect the behavior of the original video objects.

Controllable Motion Density


DropletVideo manages the pace of plot advancement and camera angle shifts by adjusting a motion control parameter. In the exemplified scenario, augmenting this parameter permits a video of equivalent length to incorporate a greater number of plot components. The video above showcases the video generation results under varying motion control parameters using identical text-image input. Under fps=8 setting, the camera's motion is apparently larger than fps=12 and fps=16, where the snowflake is seen in a perspective with a larger wide range of changes. The motion density decreases as the fps increases from 8 to 16, which confirms that using a smaller fps results into a video with much severe camera variations. This indicates that DropletVideo can proficiently modulate the playback speed of the content while preserving semantic precision.
